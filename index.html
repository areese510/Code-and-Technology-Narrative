Introduction

I am Amanda Reese. I am a teacher at Duquesne City School District. I teach 6th grade ELA and SS. I have been teaching at Duquesne for 10 years. When I was younger, I would use AOL and AIM for instant messaging to talk to friends. I use to play many computer games as a young kid. Before coming to this training I did not know much about code. When I taught 3rd grade I used Code.org before, but it was used more for independent work. I obviously used technology every day, but not to the extent we talked about the past two days. I have learned so much and how I can take it back into my ELA classroom. My mind is already working on how I can incorporate things into my ELA lessons. I have enjoyed learning more about coding especially Phoenix Code and GitHub. I think technology is always changing and advancing. Technology allows teachers to create and share content with many people.

Everyday Code

I learned that I spend a lot of time on Social Apps. I also get a lot of notifications in a day. I spend a lot of time in general on my phone. Since I am not currently working, I feel like I tend to be on my phone a lot more versus when I am working. I also compared different weeks to see if my data usage increased or decreased. When looking I found that last week’s average was about 8 hrs which was up 7% from the following week. Looking at my notifications, I noticed that last week’s average was about 391 which was up 38% from the following week. From looking at this data, I should stay off my phone a little more and turn off a lot of the notifications that don't really matter. Nowadays, people always have their phones with them or in their hands.
